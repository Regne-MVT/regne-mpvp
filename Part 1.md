# 📚 SPECIAL NOTES — FUNDAMENTAL CONCEPTS TO UNDERSTAND STRATEGIES

> This document covers the **building blocks**, **day types**, and **reversal signals** supporting the 11 strategies.

---

> 🌍 **GOLDEN RULE: RTH vs. FULL SESSION (Which Session Should I Look At?)**
> The biggest mistake in Market Profile analysis is looking at the wrong time zone. You must adjust your profile chart (TPO) according to the instrument you are trading:
> - **Indices (NQ, ES, etc.):** Only the **RTH (Regular Trading Hours / Main Session)** profile is used (e.g., 09:30 - 16:15 EST). The Asian/European overnight session (ETH) is EXCLUDED from analysis. VAH, VAL, VPOC, PDH, and PDL are derived solely from yesterday's main session.
> - **Commodities and Currencies (GC-Gold, CL-Oil, 6E-Euro):** The **FULL SESSION (23-Hour Full Day)** profile is used! Because Gold and Euro are global. Volumes generated during London and Asian hours, and VAH/VAL levels are just as valid as Wall Street and represent massive institutional walls.

# PART A: FUNDAMENTAL BUILDING BLOCKS

---

## 1. 📦 Inside Profile & Inside Day

**Definition:** A structure where the vast majority of trading volume remains within the previous day's Value Area.

```
YESTERDAY:                       TODAY (Inside Day):

High ─── 2020$                  ── Yesterday's High ── 2020$
  │                               │
  │  ── VA High ── 2015$          │  High ── 2015$  ┐
  │  ████████████                 │  ████████████   │ INSIDE!
  │  ████████████  VA             │  ████████████   │
  │  ████████████                 │  Low ─── 2005$  ┘
  │  ── VA Low ─── 2005$         │
  │                               │
Low ─── 2000$                   ── Yesterday's Low ── 2000$
```

**Example:** Yesterday gold traded between $2000 and $2020. Today, it stayed entirely between $2005 and $2015. If $2020 breaks to the upside tomorrow, we expect a sharp rally.

> **Trader's Perspective 🎯:** Inside days are the **calm before the storm.** A professional trader doesn't trade here; they wait. When the breakout occurs, the "untraded area" becomes the target.

---

## 2. ⏱️ Initial Balance — IB

**Definition:** The price range established during the first 30-60 minutes of the session. It is the day's "character test."

```
IB NARROW (expect explosion):     IB WIDE (exhausted market):

  ── IB High ──  ┐                  ── IB High ──  ┐
  │ 10 points    │ NARROW           │ 60 points    │ WIDE
  ── IB Low ───  ┘                  ── IB Low ───  ┘

→ Explosion approaching! 💥        → Market exhausted, extension unlikely 😴
```

**Rule:** If price breaks yesterday's profile → trend continuation. If it fails → sharp reversal to the opposite extreme.

> **Trader's Perspective 🎯:** If IB is very small, I expect an explosion that day. If price breaks above IB and holds, I only look for **Longs** that day.

---

## 3. 📉📈 Buying & Selling Tails

**Definition:** Price enters an area but cannot stay there — it springs back like touching a hot stove.

```
BUYING TAIL (dip):               SELLING TAIL (peak):

  ████████████                       A   ← rejected by sellers
  ████████████  body                 A
  ████████████                       AB
     A                              ABC
     A  ← rejected by buyers       ABCDE
     A  ← "this is a no-go zone!"  ABCDEF  body
```

**Rule:** When the second TPO begins, the probability of the movement continuing is very high.

**Example:** Price drops suddenly but recovers within seconds. A long tail remains on the profile = "there are strong buyers here, lower prices are rejected."

> **Trader's Perspective 🎯:** When I see a tail, the day's direction is clear. The tip of the tail is my **Stop Loss** level.

---

## 4. 🏰 Single Print Anomaly & Weekly Profile

**Definition:** Price moves so fast that no one has time to trade in that area. These gaps act like a **"magnet."**

```
SINGLE PRINT (Magnet):            WEEKLY EXTREMES (Fortress Wall):

  ████████ HVA                     ═══ Weekly HIGH ═══  ← ambush point!
     │                               │
     │  Single Print (gap)           │  as price approaches,
     │  → will be filled later!      │  institutional sellers
     │                               │  are waiting
  ████████ HVA                     ═══ Weekly LOW ════
```

> **Trader's Perspective 🎯:** I **Take Profit** when approaching the Weekly High/Low. These are institutional ambush points. If price reverses from the peak, I expect a sharp drop down to the single print gap.

# PART B: DAY TYPES

---

## 📊 Comparison of 6 Day Types

```
ENERGY COMPARISON:

Non-Trend:       😴 No energy, everyone staying put
Neutral:         ⚖️ Kicking the doors but can't get out
Normal Var.:     🏹 An arrow shot slightly in one direction
Trend (Double):  🚀 Jumping from one floor to another
Trend (Normal):  🏃💨 Door broken, everyone running one way
```

### 1. Non-Trend Day

```
IB: WIDE (60 ticks)
Extension: NONE

  ══════════════════════
  ████████████████████████  ← Stayed within wide IB
  ████████████████████████     all day
  ══════════════════════
```

| Feature | Detail |
|---------|-------|
| **IB** | Wide (60 ticks) |
| **Extension** | None |
| **Movement** | Starts before cash market opens |
| **Strategy** | Do not trade, wait |

### 2. Neutral Day — Center

```
IB: SMALL (20 ticks)
Spillover in both directions

  ↑ attack up
  ══════════
  ████████  ← IB small
  ══════════
  ↓ attack down

→ No direction, horizontal volume distribution
```

| Feature | Detail |
|---------|-------|
| **IB** | Small (20 ticks) |
| **Direction**| None — attacks in both directions |
| **Breakout** | Low probability |

### 3. Neutral Day — Extreme

Similar to Neutral Center but **the close is at the very top or very bottom.** This is a strong signal for the next day!

> **Football analogy:** Two teams draw for 90 minutes, and right at the last minute, one scores a goal. That goal is a sign that the next match will start in that team's favor.

> **Trader's Perspective 🎯:** On a Neutral Extreme close, I don't close my position; I **carry it over to tomorrow** (swing). Because the market is squeezed to the extreme, it will start aggressively in that direction tomorrow.

### 4. Normal Variation (P or b Profile)

```
P PROFILE:              b PROFILE:

████████████ ← wide     │
████████████  top        │
████████                 ████████
██                       ████████████ ← wide
█  ← narrow bottom       ████████████  bottom
```

| Feature | Detail |
|---------|-------|
| **IB** | Small |
| **Extension** | One direction |
| **Profile** | P or b shape |

> **Rocket analogy:** Launches hard (rocket), then establishes a horizontal equilibrium at the top (orbit). Buyers are in control.

### 5. Trend Day — Double Distribution

```
  ████████  ← 2nd volume area (5th floor)
  ████████
     │  ← gap (stairs, acts as S/R)
     │
  ████████  ← 1st volume area (1st floor)
  ████████
```

> **Building analogy:** Quickly climbing the stairs from the 1st floor and settling on the 5th floor. The empty stairs in between are your protection.

> **Trader's Perspective 🎯:** If price falls back into the gap between the two volume nodes → "filling" begins. If it doesn't → the trend is very strong.

### 6. Trend Day — Normal

```
  █
  ██
  ████
  ██████    ← Volume spread everywhere
  ████████     progressing with small pauses
  ██████████
  ████████████
```

Very strong directional conviction. Smashes through key levels as it advances.

> [!CAUTION]
> **NEVER DO THIS:** Never open a counter-trend trade on a Trend Day saying "it's gone up too much, it will reverse"! The market will crush you.

# PART C: REVERSAL TYPES

---

## 🔄 3 Reversal Types

### Type 1: Loss of Momentum (Less Frequent) 🐢

**Definition:** Price reaches an extreme, volume dries up. Everyone has entered positions, there is no one left behind to join.

```
LOSS OF MOMENTUM:

Price ↑
  |                    ___
  |                 __/   \  ← slowdown
  |              __/        \
  |           __/            \  ← calm reversal
  |        __/                \_
  |     __/                     \__
  |  __/                           \__
  └──────────────────────────────────→ Time

→ SHIP ANALOGY: U-turn is slow, traces a wide arc
→ Volume decreases, price lingers, then slowly turns
```

| Feature | Detail |
|---------|-------|
| **Speed** | Slow — "turning a ship takes time" |
| **Volume** | Decreases, dries up |
| **Duration**| Long |
| **Control change**| Calm, gradual |

> **Trader's Perspective 🎯:** I don't press "sell" immediately. I wait for the ship to turn. I watch the price "spin its wheels" with low volume, and enter the game when the rotation accelerates.

### Type 2: Excess (Frequent) 🔥

**Definition:** The market moves rapidly to an extreme with low volume, then snaps back just as fast — **V-Reversal.**

```
EXCESS (V-REVERSAL):

Price ↑
  |
  |  \                    /
  |   \                  /
  |    \                /
  |     \              /
  |      \            /
  |       \    ↙ tail
  |        \__/  ← "touching a hot stove and pulling back"
  |
  └────────────────────────→ Time

→ BOW ANALOGY: The further you pull back, the faster it returns
→ Leaves a "tail" at the extreme
```

| Feature | Detail |
|---------|-------|
| **Speed** | Very fast — V shape |
| **Volume** | Low at the extreme |
| **Signal** | Leaves a tail |
| **Duration**| Short |

> **Trader's Perspective 🎯:** If price is rising fast but volume is low and a tail forms, this is NOT a buying opportunity — it's a "come hither" trap by professionals!

### Type 3: Aggressive Reversal (Strongest) 💥

**Definition:** A combination of Volatility + Volume + Overpricing. The strongest tops and bottoms form here.

```
AGGRESSIVE REVERSAL:

Price ↑
  |
  |  \
  |   \  high volatility
  |    \ (fast drop)
  |     \
  |      \💥 CRASH!  ← high volume wall
  |      /  (institutional orders)
  |     /
  |    /  sharp bounce back
  |   /
  |  /
  └────────────────────────→ Time

→ WALL ANALOGY: A ball hits a concrete wall fast and bounces back
→ Loss of momentum + Volatility + Volume all together
```

| Feature | Detail |
|---------|-------|
| **Speed** | Very fast (in both directions) |
| **Volume** | Very high (at the reversal point) |
| **Volatility**| Very high |
| **R/R** | HIGHEST opportunities! |

> **Trader's Perspective 🎯:** My stop level is crystal clear: the very tip of the tail. If price breaks that, my scenario is canceled. If it doesn't, my target is the other end of the profile — **highest R/R.**

---

## 📊 Comparison of 3 Reversal Types

| Feature | Loss of Momentum 🐢 | Excess 🔥 | Aggressive 💥 |
|---------|-------------------|-----------|-----------|
| **Frequency** | Low | High | Medium |
| **Speed** | Slow | Fast | Very fast |
| **Volume** | Decreases | Low at extreme | Very high at reversal |
| **Signal** | Spinning wheels, slowdown | Tail | Volatility + volume + tail |
| **Patience** | Highly required | Medium | Low (quick decision) |
| **R/R** | Medium | Good | BEST |
| **Analogy** | Turning a ship | Touching hot stove | Hitting a wall |

## 💡 GENERAL TRADER MINDSET

1. **Recognize day type early:** Determine the character of the day in the first hour — this dictates your strategy selection
2. **IB is your compass:** Narrow IB = expect explosion. Wide IB = apply Str.6
3. **Tails tell you direction:** Tail = rejection. Trade in the opposite direction of the tail
4. **Single print = magnet:** Gaps are filled later — use them as targets
5. **Watch weekly extremes:** Institutional ambush points — take profit zones
6. **Read reversal type correctly:** Is it a ship, a stove, or a wall? Each requires different entry timing
7. **DO NOT fade a Trend day:** This is the most expensive mistake

> [!TIP]
> **Build a morning routine:**
> 1. Review yesterday's profile (what is the day type?)
> 2. Measure IB width (compare with SMA)
> 3. Mark tails and single prints
> 4. Map Weekly High/Lows
> 5. Which strategy(ies) can be applied today? → PLAN
