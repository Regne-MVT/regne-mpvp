
# 📚 SPECIAL NOTES 3 — THE 20 GOLDEN RULES OF PROFESSIONALS (JIM DALTON)

> The secret wisdom and 20 landmark lessons — rule-grade insights from professionals like Jim Dalton, Peter Steidlmayer, and Axia Futures — that transform Market Profile from an ordinary indicator into the art of reading the market's soul.

---

### 1. Price Is Just an Advertisement — "Value" Is What Matters

Jim Dalton says: *"Price is like a 'Big Sale!' sign hanging in a store window. It's only an advertisement to attract customers."*
**Example:** Gold suddenly spikes to $2,050 but stays there for only 2 seconds before dropping back to $2,040. $2,050 is just an advertisement.

> 💡 **Note:** Don't be fooled by the instantaneous price movements (Price Spikes) you see on screen. What matters is whether price spends time in that area and prints letters there (Acceptance). Real value is where volume (Value Area) accumulates.

**🎯 Action:** When price suddenly rockets to a key resistance, don't open a Long out of FOMO (fear). Wait at least 1–2 periods (30–60 min) for TPOs to print at that price. If they can't, hunt a Reversal (Short) in the opposite direction.

---

### 2. A Breakout Is NOT "Crossing the Line" (The Acceptance Rule)

The average trader thinks a candle closing above a line means it's "broken." According to Dalton, a breakout is when price crosses that line and then spends time there, creating new volume.
**Example:** Price exceeds yesterday's high (PDH), and letters A, B, C begin stacking (TPOs printing) side by side at that level.

> 💡 **Note:** When resistance is broken, don't immediately shout "We're flying!" Wait to see if price accepts and holds there (Acceptance). If it only spikes and reverses, that's a fake breakout (Rejection).

**🎯 Action:** Wait for "Confirmation" after a resistance break. When the newly forming Value Area begins shifting above the broken line (Value Migration), join the Long trade.

---

### 3. Time Is the Most Ruthless Filter

*"If price truly wants to break a major resistance, it won't linger there for hours!"* The longer price stalls, the less likely a breakout becomes.
**Example:** Price attacks the 4500 resistance for 3 hours (6 TPO periods) but can't get through. It ultimately collapses downward.

> 💡 **Note:** Real buyers (OTF Buyers), when hungry, will break it in seconds. Excessive stalling at resistance (Time Consolidation) signals buyer weakness and hints that the trend will reverse (Fade/Reversal).

**🎯 Action:** If price has been at resistance for more than 4 periods (2 hours) without breaking above (forming a Poor/Weak High), open a trade in the opposite direction (Short) right below the resistance, with a stop 3–4 ticks above it.

---

### 4. Never Stand in Front of "One Time Framing"

If every half-hour letter (TPO) is printing above the previous one (One Time Framing), one side has completely captured the market.
**Example:** The low of candle C is higher than the low of B; the low of D is higher than C. Price climbs like a staircase.

> 💡 **Note:** Trying to short (Counter-trend) a market that is stairstepping upward (One Time Framing Up) is suicide. Don't fight the institutional wind (OTF Initiative) — ride it.

**🎯 Action:** As long as you see One Time Framing Up on the profile, ABSOLUTELY DO NOT look for Shorts (Sells). Only go Long on pullbacks at yesterday's VPOC or VWAP levels.

---

### 5. "P" and "b" Profiles Are Perfect Traps

If the profile shows a bulge at the top (P) or at the bottom (b) at day's end, amateurs assume the trend will continue.
**Example:** After a falling morning session, the market suddenly rallies in the afternoon, forming a "P" shape at the top.

> 💡 **Note:** A P profile is not a real rally — it's a panic-driven short covering (Short Covering) by those who sold in the morning and are now closing losing positions. There are no fresh buyers. Typically, this artificial rally unwinds the next day (Liquidation Break).

**🎯 Action:** If the daily close forms a clear "P" shape, watch for opportunities to sell (Short) any morning spikes the next day. The trade becomes profitable once the midpoint of the P breaks down.

---

### 6. Single Prints (LVA) Are NEVER "Required to Be Filled"!

When a Single Print (a thin gap between letters) forms during the day, amateurs open counter-trend positions expecting it to be filled.
**Example:** Price jumps from 4000 to 4020, leaving a thin column of only 'D' letters in between.

> 💡 **Note:** That gap (Single Print / Low Volume Area) is proof that institutions were aggressively buying. The market is NOT required to fill that gap. On the contrary, trades should be opened in the direction of the gap (Trend Day).

**🎯 Action:** When price pulls back and touches the upper boundary of the thin gap (Single Print), the gap acts as support. The moment price touches the gap, open a trade in the direction of the main trend.

---

### 7. Blunt Tops and Bottoms (Poor High/Low) Will Definitely Be Repaired

If the session high/low has no sharp spike (wick), and letters are lined up side by side creating a flat boundary — the auction is not yet over.
**Example:** At the day's highest price (e.g., 4200.00), letters A, C, E, and F are arranged in a flat, blunt wall.

> 💡 **Note:** The market dislikes flat tops drawn with a ruler (Poor High/Low). That peak is weak, and in the coming days the market will return to "repair" (break through) it.

**🎯 Action:** If you're looking for a Take Profit level in an uptrend, and there's a leftover "Poor High" from a prior day above, place your limit order exactly 2–3 ticks ABOVE that blunt top.

---

### 8. If You See a Ledge (Brick Wall), Prepare for a Waterfall

If 4–5 different letters line up like a ruler at exactly the same price, that is not natural.
**Example:** While price is falling, it bounces precisely off the 4150.25 level across 5 separate TPO periods to the millimeter.

> 💡 **Note:** This artificial wall (Ledge) is the result of weak buyers stacking limit orders there. The moment this wall breaks, the massive stop losses (Stop Loss) beneath it will detonate, and price will cascade (Stop Run / Liquidation) downward like a waterfall.

**🎯 Action:** Place a "Stop Buy / Stop Sell" (breakout order) just below (or above) the Ledge. When that wall breaks, ride the resulting 20–30 point Stop-Run move for tremendous profit.

---

### 9. IB (First 1 Hour) Is the Day's DNA

The price range of the first hour is set by local/day traders.
**Example:** During the first hour (A and B periods), price oscillates between 100 and 120 (IB Range). At 11:00 AM, price rockets to 125.

> 💡 **Note:** When price breaks the Initial Balance (first 1-hour range) up or down (Range Extension), it signals that longer-term institutional players (OTF) with much deeper pockets have entered the market.

**🎯 Action:** If the morning IB range is very narrow (Inside Range), lay in ambush for the breakout. When the breakout arrives and new TPOs hold in that direction (Acceptance), aggressively enter in the direction of the IB breakout.

---

### 10. When Balance Is Broken, the Market Goes Crazy

If the market has been oscillating inside yesterday's Value Area for several days, it is "In Balance."
**Example:** Price stays strictly between 4000–4050 for 3 consecutive days. On day 4, it breaks above 4060 and rockets.

> 💡 **Note:** The longer the balance/consolidation (Balance/Consolidation) lasts, the more the spring is compressed. When balance breaks (Breakout from Balance), one of the year's sharpest and most profitable moves begins.

**🎯 Action:** When price exits a 3–4 day range, DO NOT open a counter-trade thinking "It'll come back!" Take a position in the breakout direction and target at minimum the size of yesterday's Value Area in profit.

---

### 11. Weekly Extremes Are Institutional Ambush Points

When the market reaches its weekly high or low, retail traders excitedly open trades expecting a breakout.
**Example:** Price reaches the lowest point of the prior week (Weekly Low). While amateurs are selling, price suddenly springs back from there.

> 💡 **Note:** Weekly Extremes are massive support/resistance levels where the whales lie in wait. As long as they don't break, extremely violent reversals (Reversal / Rejection) occur from these levels.

**🎯 Action:** When price touches the Weekly Low for the first time, don't blindly sell. The moment price rejects the level and leaves an upper spike (Tail), go Long and ride it to weekly resistance.

---

### 12. Gaps (Gap Opens) Are the Most Valuable Opportunities

If the market opens with a gap far outside yesterday's range, market perception changed overnight.
**Example:** The market closed at 4000 yesterday and opens today at 4050. In the first half hour, instead of returning to 4000, it continues to 4060.

> 💡 **Note:** If the gap doesn't immediately close (Gap Fill Fail), massive new money has entered the market. In these situations, aggressive trades (Open Drive) are only opened in the direction of the gap.

**🎯 Action:** If a market that gapped open in the morning fails to return to fill the gap in the first half hour (A period), apply the "Go-With" (ride the flow) strategy and trade in the direction of the gap (e.g., Long).

---

### 13. The Middle of a Double Distribution Is the Safest Foxhole

If price has built volume at two separate zones during the day and the middle is empty, that middle is a foxhole.
**Example:** The profile is fat at the bottom, fat at the top, but has a very thin letter column (Single Print) in between.

> 💡 **Note:** If price starts falling from the upper bell, that middle gap (LVA / Low Volume Area) acts as an incredibly solid support. Price hits the gap and reverses back up (Support at LVA).

**🎯 Action:** When price relaxes from the upper distribution, go Long right at the boundary where the thin middle gap begins. Place your Stop Loss just behind the boundary where the thin gap ends and the lower bell begins. Risk is very small.

---

### 14. VPOC Is Just a Magnet, NOT Support/Resistance!

Amateurs open trades when price comes near the day's thickest line (VPOC).
**Example:** Price reaches the densest volume (VPOC) red line from the morning session and spends the rest of the day sawing back and forth around that line.

> 💡 **Note:** VPOC (Volume Point of Control) is a black hole. It attracts price toward itself (Magnet Effect), but once price is inside it, what happens next is uncertain. Never OPEN a new trade at the VPOC — TAKE PROFIT there.

**🎯 Action:** When price reaches the VPOC line, take profit on your existing position (Long/Short) and step aside. Opening a new trade around the VPOC is like flipping a coin. Wait for price to move away from it.

---

### 15. The "Open" Is the Day's Zero Point

The opening price is not just an ordinary number.
**Example:** The market opens at 4100. Throughout the day it climbs to 4150 and never revisits 4100 again.

> 💡 **Note:** Price never returning to where it opened (No Return to Open) signals a single-directional, powerful conviction in the market. It's the biggest secret code of Trend Days.

**🎯 Action:** If it's 11:00 AM and price has still never dipped below the opening price, that day should be declared a "Long Only" day. Use every pullback as a buying opportunity.

---

### 16. The Oxygen Rule (Volume-Less Breakout)

Price is making new highs, but the Volume Profile at those peaks shows "no volume," and the letters are sparse...
**Example:** Price breaks above 4500 and flies to 4520, but the volume profile at 4520 shows only 20 lots of transactions.

> 💡 **Note:** If trading volume dries up at new highs (Low Volume Exhaustion), buyers have pushed price up there but no one has "accepted" buying at that price. Price runs out of oxygen and collapses back down (to the VPOC).

**🎯 Action:** If Order Flow (volume) goes to zero at a new high, open a Short (Sell) just below that thin, sparse structure at the peak and target the thickest area of the day (VPOC).

---

### 17. Do What the "Auction" Says, Not What Your Emotions Say

Forget the feeling of "I think it'll reverse here" — look at how the letters are arranged.
**Example:** Price has been falling all day. You're thinking "It's gotten so cheap" inside, but you're below yesterday's Value Area (Value Area).

> 💡 **Note:** If price is below yesterday's Value, sellers have taken initiative (Initiative Selling). As long as the auction continues, there is no concept of "cheap/expensive" — follow the letters and the trend (Market Flow).

**🎯 Action:** As long as we remain below yesterday's VAL (Value Area Low), resist the urge inside you to go Long (Buy). Only open a Long trade if price breaks back above yesterday's VAL and gets accepted there.

---

### 18. The Close Is the Next Day's Signal

If 70% of the trades happened in the middle but price closes at the very bottom, sellers have won.
**Example:** We oscillated around VPOC (4050) all day, but the market suddenly dropped to 4020 (the very low) right at the close and ended there.

> 💡 **Note:** If value is in the middle but the close is at an extreme (Neutral Extreme Day), a large institution slammed the table at the last moment. The next morning, the market will most likely open in the direction of that close (with a decline).

**🎯 Action:** If a "Neutral Extreme" day has occurred, aggressively chase trades in the direction of that extreme close (Trade the Extension) when the market opens the next morning.

---

### 19. Rejection Tails Are Made for Trade Entry

If price slams into a level and reverses within seconds leaving a massive spike/tail, that level is sacred.
**Example:** Price touches 4000 support, and within seconds springs back to 4015. A long letter tail (Tail) is left behind.

> 💡 **Note:** This is the "touch the hot stove and run" move. That long tail (Buying Tail) shows that the price was deemed unfair. Enter a trade (Reversal Trade) immediately in the direction of the bounce, using the tip of the tail as your Stop Loss.

**🎯 Action:** The moment a long Buying Tail appears on screen, immediately enter a Long position even if price hasn't yet pulled back. Place your stop exactly 1 tick below the tip of the tail (Extreme).

---

### 20. Weakness Is Contagious (Structural Weakness)

If there are 4–5 different "Anomalies" in a profile, that trend is rotten.
**Example:** The profile is trending up but behind it are a Blunt Top (Poor High), an unfilled Gap (Single Print), and a Ledge (Wall).

> 💡 **Note:** If a profile is riddled with anomalies (Anomalies), that rally is hollow (Poor Structure). Institutions will suddenly crash price back to where it came from to repair these structural weaknesses (Repair the Structure). Lay in ambush!

**🎯 Action:** If the profile has risen in a riddled, hollow fashion (Poor Structure), once price first runs out of gas (goes oxygen-less), open a massive Short position — because the target is to clean all those anomalies downward in a single day.

## SECTION 2: 20 MAJOR RULES OF PETER STEIDLMAYER AND AXIA FUTURES

### 21. There Are Only Two Phases in the Market (Steidlmayer)

The creator of Market Profile, Steidlmayer, says: *"The market only does two things: It either trends (seeks a new value), or enters balance (buys and sells at the value it found)."*
**Example:** Price stays between 4000–4050 for 3 days (Balance). On day 4, it rockets to 4100 (Trend).

> 💡 **Note:** Applying a trend strategy inside a balance zone (Balance Area) is suicide. Knowing which phase (Trend vs Balance) the market is currently in is more important than anything else.

**🎯 Action:** On balance days (Balance), only buy dips and sell peaks (Fade the Extremes). On trend days, take positions only in pullbacks in that direction.

---

### 22. The Famous "80% Rule" (Axia Futures)

If price enters yesterday's Value Area (VA) from outside, and  **remains inside for more than half an hour (Acceptance)** , there is an 80% probability it will travel all the way to the other end.
**Example:** Price falls inside yesterday's Value Area High (VAH), and prints two letters (A and B) there. The target is now yesterday's Value Area Low (VAL).

> 💡 **Note:** This rule (80% Rule / Value Area Rule) is Prop Firms' favorite trade. The target is always the opposite shore of the Value Area.

**🎯 Action:** If price drops below VAH from outside and remains there for 2 consecutive periods (1 hour), place your Stop just above VAH and Short directly toward the VAL (Value Area Low) target.

---

### 23. Asymmetric Opportunities Are Hidden at the Extremes (Steidlmayer)

You make money not by trading exactly at the middle of the bell curve (VPOC), but by entering the market at the **outermost edges (Extremes)** of the bell curve.
**Example:** Entering a trade at the VPOC is coin-flipping. Entering a trade by selling from the weekly high (Weekly High) is an asymmetric opportunity.

> 💡 **Note:** At the extremes (Extremes), your Stop Loss distance is very short (low risk), but your profit target (all the way to the VPOC) is very large (high reward). The Risk/Reward (R/R) ratio is excellent at the extremes.

**🎯 Action:** When looking for trades, always have your eyes on the edges of the map (Extremes). Sell from the upper edge of the profile, buy from the lower edge — never enter a trade from the middle (VPOC).

---

### 24. Volume Confirms or Lies About Price (Steidlmayer)

If price is climbing to new highs but the volume/letter (TPO) count forming there is much smaller than before, the move has been "Denied."
**Example:** When price was at 4500, there were 5,000 lots traded. When price moves to 4520, there are only 200 lots.

> 💡 **Note:** The big player found that price too expensive and stopped buying (Volume Divergence). A U-turn (Reversal) is near.

**🎯 Action:** If TPOs at a new high are very sparse (Low Volume Node), aggressively Short below that weak top (as long as it's not broken), because the buyers have run out of oxygen.

---

### 25. "Sponge" Zones / Absorption (Axia Futures)

Sometimes very aggressive sellers enter the market, push price down, but price just won't fall!
**Example:** On the Order Flow (Footprint) screen, thousands of red "Sell" orders flood in, but price won't drop even from 4000.00 to 3999.75.

> 💡 **Note:** Because a massive passive buyer (Limit Buyer) waiting below is absorbing all those sells like a sponge (Absorption). The moment the sellers' ammo (Momentum) runs out, price springs up like a compressed spring.

**🎯 Action:** Don't be fooled by the heavy red sells on screen. If price isn't falling (Absorption), the moment sellers are exhausted, follow the Whale — enter Long with a Market Order, stop placed 1 tick below the Whale's order.

---

### 26. High Volume Nodes (HVN) Are Safe Harbors (Steidlmayer)

The thick, belly-shaped zones (HVN) in a profile are safe harbors. When price enters one of these areas, it slows down, becomes sticky, and lingers.
**Example:** While price is falling rapidly, it enters a thick zone (HVN) where it spent hours yesterday, and the drop suddenly stops.

> 💡 **Note:** If you are going to take profit (Take Profit) in a trade, your target should always be these swollen areas (High Volume Node). Price becomes "sticky" here.

**🎯 Action:** If you're in a position and considering taking profit, place your Take Profit order right in front of the next massive belly (HVN) ahead of price. Price will pause when it touches it.

---

### 27. Low Volume Nodes (LVN) Are Highways (Steidlmayer)

The thin, narrow spots (gaps) in a profile are like highways. Price never slows down when it enters one.
**Example:** Price enters a thin LVA (Low Volume Area) formed yesterday and shoots 20 points in 1 minute.

> 💡 **Note:** Trades should not be opened inside these thin zones (LVN) because they move too fast and will blast your stop. That area is only a "fast transit" (Fast Transit) zone.

**🎯 Action:** NEVER open a trade right INSIDE (in the middle of) an LVN. If you must open one, do it either at the entry toll (start of LVN) or exit toll (end of LVN) of the highway.

---

### 28. "Trapped Participants" Move the Market (Axia Futures)

Axia traders scan the screen asking: *"Who is trapped right now and suffering?"*
**Example:** Price breaks through resistance so everyone opens "Long (Buy)" — but within seconds price falls back below the resistance. Everyone who went Long is now trapped (Trapped Longs).

> 💡 **Note:** The real big money is made by trading in the opposite direction of those panicking traders (Stop hunting / Stop Run). Watch the trapped participants.

**🎯 Action:** When price breaks a resistance and immediately falls back below it, declare "This is a Fakeout" and immediately Short. Because the exploding stops of those trapped above will become your profit.

---

### 29. Don't Trade the News — Trade the "Reaction to the News" (Axia Futures)

An interest rate decision or Non-Farm Payroll number may have come in very positive.
**Example:** The data comes in unbelievably good, price jumps 50 points, but 10 minutes later price collapses back inside yesterday's boundaries (Value Area).

> 💡 **Note:** No matter how good the news is, the rejection (Rejection) in the profile is real. The market has "Sold the News."

**🎯 Action:** No matter how good the data is, if price is sharply rejected (leaving a Tail) at yesterday's high (PDH), ignore the news, follow the technicals, and sell (Short).

---

### 30. The Sacred Formula: Price + Time = Volume (Steidlmayer)

What creates volume in the market is not price moving to a place. It is price spending **"Time"** at the place it travels to.
**Example:** If price touches 4000 for 1 second, there will be 10 lots of volume. But if it waits at 4000 for 2 hours, there will be 100,000 lots.

> 💡 **Note:** Without Time, there is no Volume. Without Volume, a breakout is fake (Fakeout).

**🎯 Action:** When a support/resistance breaks, don't make a decision within seconds. Look for at least 30 minutes (1 TPO) of price spending time at that level, then join the trade.

---

### 31. The Spike Rule (Last-Minute Surge)

If price suddenly fires in one direction (Spike) 30 minutes before the market close, we'll know if it's real the next morning.
**Example:** Right at close, price rockets into space (Spike up). The next morning, price opens below the spike's peak.

> 💡 **Note:** If it opens below the spike (or in the opposite direction), that last-minute surge was a complete lie (Rejected Spike). If it opens inside or above it, the spike was real (Accepted Spike).

**🎯 Action:** If yesterday's closing candle had a "Spike Up" and today we're opening BELOW that candle, the buyers who created that spike are underwater. Aggressively Short at the open.

---

### 32. Price Never Forgets Where It Was Rejected (Axia Futures)

If yesterday price came to 4500 and was violently slammed down from there (long tail), when it returns today, its memory kicks in.
**Example:** As price approaches 4500 again, everyone is poised to hit the sell button unless it can quickly blast through.

> 💡 **Note:** Don't blindly open a trend-following (Long) trade as price approaches an area of very strong prior rejection (Strong Rejection/Tail).

**🎯 Action:** As price approaches yesterday's long Tail tip, take all your profits (Long). The moment price rejects there again, place your Stop very close to the tip and enter the opposite trade (Short).

---

### 33. An Inside Day Is a Compressed Spring (Steidlmayer)

If all of today's letters fit within yesterday's range (Range), the market is accumulating energy.
**Example:** Monday's price range is 100–150. Tuesday's range is only 110–130 (Inside Day).

> 💡 **Note:** The next day (Wednesday) is the strongest candidate for the day when that spring releases and a very sharp trend (Trend Day Breakout) begins.

**🎯 Action:** The day after an Inside Day, whichever direction price breaks out of yesterday's narrow range, open a large position in that direction (Go-With) and hold at least until the close (Trend Trade).

---

### 34. Strength Requires Proof, Weakness Cannot Be Hidden (Steidlmayer)

In an upward market, price must continuously create volume at new highs to prove its strength.
**Example:** Price is going up, but the thickest line (VPOC) always stays below.

> 💡 **Note:** If the profile's belly (Value) is not following price, that is a clear weakness (Divergence). The structure is rotting from within.

**🎯 Action:** If price has gained 50 points but the VPOC hasn't moved from yesterday, take profit on Long trades and stay on guard. At the slightest reversal candle (Reversal), set an ambush for a Short.

### 35. Identify the "Other Time Frame" Player (OTF)

Regular intraday traders (Local traders) keep price within the Initial Balance (IB) of the first hour.
**Example:** Price stays in a 50-point range until noon. Then at 1:00 PM it suddenly breaks that 50-point range and never looks back.

> 💡 **Note:** If price breaks the IB boundaries and keeps going in that direction (Range Extension), we say *"Long-term institutions with billions (OTF) in their pocket have entered the game."* You cannot stand against them.

**🎯 Action:** When IB breaks to the upside, instead of saying "It'll reverse, let me Short," join the OTF (Big Money) trend. Use the breakout point as support and go Long.

---

### 36. Opening Type (Open Type) Determines the Day's Fate

The opening of the first 15 minutes sets the psychology for the rest of the day.
**Example:** The moment the market opens, price quickly escapes beyond yesterday's boundaries and never looks back (Open Drive).

> 💡 **Note:** When you see an "Open Drive," trade only in that direction all day (Trend Day). If price oscillates back and forth at the open (Open Auction), it's a boring "Balance" day.

**🎯 Action:** In a morning "Open Drive" (rockets from the open) market, at the slightest breath (pause in letters), dive in with a market order in that direction, and ride the flow.

---

### 37. Emotions Deceive, TPOs (Letters) Never Do (Steidlmayer)

Saying "I think the market has dropped too much, it'll reverse from here" is gambling. A Market Profile trader never says "I think."
**Example:** Price looks very cheap, but the letters (TPO) keep printing below yesterday's levels.

> 💡 **Note:** If letters are printing lower, value is falling (Value is Lower). Throw your emotions (Emotions) in the trash — only sell value.

**🎯 Action:** If new TPO letters are closing below the previous one, NEVER look for "Buy the Dip" entries. Your system should be built entirely on opening Shorts from resistances.

---

### 38. Let It Confirm (Confirmation vs Anticipation)

The first rule taught to Axia traders: Don't enter a position in advance expecting a breakout!
**Example:** A resistance looks like it's about to break, you buy immediately to "not miss it" (Anticipation), but price reverses.

> 💡 **Note:** Let price break the line. Let letters begin accumulating there (Acceptance/Confirmation). Enter after you get confirmation. You'll miss 10% of the gain, but you'll protect your capital 90% of the time.

**🎯 Action:** Instead of entering a "Limit Buy" hoping for a breakout, wait for the breakout to occur and for at least 2 letters (TPO) to print there, then buy on the pullback. (Get acceptance confirmation).

---

### 39. Use Poor Tops as "Targets"

Say there's a blunt (weak) top (Poor High) above and price is trading lower.
**Example:** You caught a Long from the bottom — now you're asking "Where should I sell?" There's a flat "Poor High" from yesterday above.

> 💡 **Note:** The exact place to take profit (Take Profit) is just a few ticks above where that weak top above is broken (Repaired/Repaired).

**🎯 Action:** "Poor High" or "Poor Low" lines left from prior days are those perfect "magnet" points where you place your Take Profit (Limit) orders. Write your limit order right on top of them.

---

### 40. Trading Is NOT a "Being Right" Game

Professionals are there to make money, not to be proven right.
**Example:** You go Long expecting an upside move, but price pokes outside (below) yesterday's Value Area (breakout fails).

> 💡 **Note:** The moment you realize you're in a trade in the wrong direction, don't let your ego stop you — stop out immediately and cut your losses (Cut Losses Fast). Saying "It'll come back, I know it" is an amateur/gambler disease.

**🎯 Action:** Before entering a trade, draw the line that says "If this level breaks, I'm wrong" (Invalidation Level). The moment price touches that line, close the position without questioning.

## SECTION 3: 20 KEY RULES FROM "MIND OVER MARKETS" (JIM DALTON)

### 41. The Market Is a Perfect Auction

Dalton's most fundamental philosophy: The sole purpose of the market is to bring buyers and sellers together to facilitate "trading."
**Example:** If nobody is buying at 4500 (no volume), the market lowers the price to 4400 and waits there until it finds buyers.

> 💡 **Note:** Price is only an advertisement (Advertisement). The real goal is to find the point — Volume/Value — where the most buyers and sellers agree.

**🎯 Action:** If price climbs to a high level like 4500 but the letters (TPO) vanish within seconds, the price "advertisement" has failed. Immediately switch to selling (Short).

---

### 42. Follow the Footprints of the Big Money (Other Timeframe)

There are two types of players in the market: Day Traders (day-trippers) and Long-Term Institutional Players (Other Timeframe).
**Example:** In the first hour of the morning, the market oscillates in a very tight range. Then suddenly price fires and rises all day.

> 💡 **Note:** Day traders keep the market within the Initial Balance (first 1-hour range). If that range breaks, the "Big Money" (OTF) has taken the stage. Your only job is to follow the OTF's direction.

**🎯 Action:** The second you see an IB (First 1 Hour) Range Extension (Breakout), OTF is in play. From that point on, stop saying "It's risen/fallen too much" — aggressively enter in the direction of the breakout.

---

### 43. Initiative vs. Responsive Trading

If price is moving ABOVE yesterday's Value Area (VA) and finding buyers there, this is an "Initiative" buy.
**Example:** Yesterday price was between 100–110. Today price went to 115 and people are still buying.

> 💡 **Note:** When people are still buying even though price is "expensive," that's aggression (Initiative Buying). Buying a falling price because it's "cheap" is merely a reaction (Responsive Buying). Trends are started by Initiative moves.

**🎯 Action:** When price holds above yesterday's VAH (Value Area High) (Initiative Buying), don't Short saying "It'll pull back from here." Use pullbacks as Long (Buy) opportunities.

---

### 44. TPO (Letter) Is an Opportunity Imbalance

The more TPOs (letters) that stack side by side at a price, the more "Fair" the market finds that price.
**Example:** At 4050, 15 different letters are lined up side by side. At 4100, there is only 1 letter.

**Example:** While price falls, the sell delta (reds) drops from -500, -300, to -50, and suddenly a +200 buy delta (green) appears.

> 💡 **Note:** Let price fall. See on the Order Flow screen that sellers are exhausted and buyers are sliding under price like a spatula (Shift in Momentum / Delta Flip) with your own eyes, then enter.

**🎯 Action:** When price approaches support, don't directly enter a limit buy. Go Long only AFTER you see with your own eyes that those big Red sells have flipped to Green (Momentum Shift) on the Order Flow screen.

### 92. Unaccepted Tops Are Always Retested

This is the Order Flow equivalent of the Poor High (Blunt Top) concept.
**Example:** At the very peak, instead of single-digit "0" or "1" lot, 500 lots of volume are stuck.

> 💡 **Note:** High volume remaining at the top shows that the area was "not rejected" (No Excess / Poor Extreme). A pause has been made — that top will definitely be attacked again.

**🎯 Action:** If a high volume residue (e.g., 300 in the top box) remains at the top on the Footprint, that top is weak. It counts as already exceeded. Continue Long trades with the target of "it will be exceeded."

### 93. Pre-Close Delta Poisoning

Just because delta closed very highly positive at day's end doesn't mean "It'll definitely go up tomorrow."
**Example:** The market fell all day, but in the last half hour makes +2000 delta (Buy), yet price barely rises.

> 💡 **Note:** That delta might be sellers' buybacks to take profit (Short Covering). Understanding "Who" (Institutional vs Retail) created the volume is essential.

**🎯 Action:** After 3:30 PM (at close), don't be tricked by suddenly surging green deltas into saying "It'll definitely fly tomorrow." If price isn't going up but delta is green, it's just Short Covering — it doesn't change direction.

### 94. Aggressive Orders Create Trends

On trend days, trying to enter with passive limit orders (Let me wait for a pullback) causes you to miss the train.
**Example:** The market is rallying, you say "Let it come back to VPOC and I'll buy," but price never comes back.

> 💡 **Note:** When OTF (Big Money) takes initiative, it attacks with market orders (Aggressive Market Orders) and doesn't wait. On strong days, don't wait for a pullback — jump on the train.

**🎯 Action:** The moment you sense a trend day (80% rule) has started, throw away the "Wait for it to relax first" strategy. That day is the day to plunge into the market with a market order — be aggressive.

### 95. Every Trade Is an Assumption

Market Profile doesn't show you the future. It tells you "the probability given the current situation."
**Example:** Price fires up leaving a Single Print. You go Long with the Assumption "An uptrend has started."

> 💡 **Note:** If price seconds later crushes that Single Print and reverses downward, the assumption is busted (Invalidation). A real trader doesn't insist — they immediately reverse.

**🎯 Action:** Before entering a trade, draw the "If this level is exceeded, the scenario is CANCELLED (Invalidation)" line on your chart. When price hits that line, cut the loss without thinking about anything.

### 96. Choose Weak Opponents (Fade the Weak)

Axia traders don't fight strong trends. They look for "weakness" in the market.
**Example:** Just because news comes, amateurs panic and form a P profile from the bottom (Short Covering).

> 💡 **Note:** Professionals, the moment that weak P profile gives a reversal signal from the top, descend on those amateurs (Weak Hands) with full force (Fade).

**🎯 Action:** When you see an artificial rally (P profile, sparse letters) created by amateurs (OTF), not professionals, on your screen — boldly open a Short trade against those weak hands.

### 97. The Best Trades Are the Most Frightening Ones

Playing a reversal from a spike while price is falling rapidly without support is frightening.
**Example:** While the market is bleeding, going Long from a key weekly support (Weekly LVA).

### 98. There Is No "Certainty" in the Market — There Are Probabilities

None of the rules work 100% of the time. If the "80% Rule" is working, trying it 10 times means winning 8 of them.
**Example:** You apply the 80% rule and stop out 2 times in a row, then abandon the strategy saying "This doesn't work."

> 💡 **Note:** Instead of dumping the strategy (Edge) when you lose, you should say "I stopped out within probabilities (Probabilities) — moving on to the next trade."

**🎯 Action:** When you apply Market Profile rules and stop out (lose money), NEVER break the rules and go outside the system. Because the system will make you profitable over 10 trades.

### 99. The Holy Grail Is "Context"

Neither Market Profile, nor Order Flow, nor technical analysis alone is the Holy Grail.
**Example:** You can have the best indicator in the world, but if you don't know whether the market is currently in Balance (Consolidation) or Trend (Imbalance) phase, you'll lose money.

> 💡 **Note:** The Holy Grail is your ability to use these tools to correctly read the big picture of the market (Context / Context).

**🎯 Action:** In the first 5 minutes of sitting down at the screen, don't touch your mouse. Only seek the answer to "Are we in Balance today (Saw), or in Trend (Breakout)?" Everything after that is just pulling the trigger.
